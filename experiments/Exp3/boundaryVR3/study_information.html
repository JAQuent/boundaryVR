The aim of this study is to understand how our brains process information in a spatial context. In this experiment you will be shown a video depicting navigation through rooms and will then be asked questions about the video. Prior to starting, we test your connection speed and browser so we can verify that the movies can be played. The whole session should last around est_time minutes. You may take short breaks, but bear in mind you must finish the session within max_time minutes.

Anonymised electronic research data will be stored on the hosting site’s servers that are compliant with EU data protection and security policies, and data transfer will be encrypted.

We will not have access to identifying information (PID) like name, IP address or bank details; only demographic information (e.g. age, gender, language).

In addition to your responses during the study, we collect information about the device, operating system and web browser that you’re using. This will not include any information that would identify you.

This study uses cookies - small packets of information sent by our server and stored by your web browser. Some of these will be deleted automatically when you close your browser, others will remain after you close your browser. These persistent cookies can be removed manually using your browser settings. Cookies are necessary in order for the experiment to run properly and will not contain any sensitive or personal information. By taking part you agree to the use of cookies. For more information please see https://studies.mrc-cbu.cam.ac.uk/privacy.html"

Cambridge University is the sponsor for this study based in the United Kingdom. We will be using information from you in order to undertake this study and will act as the data controller for this. Your rights to access, change or move your information are limited, as we need to manage your information in specific ways in order for the research to be reliable and accurate.
